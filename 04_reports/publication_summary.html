Abstract

This paper examines the stock market response to ESG rating changes and investigates how state-level environmental policies moderate these effects. Using a comprehensive sample of 453,748 firm-month observations from 2010-2024, we employ an event study methodology with multiple factor model specifications to measure abnormal returns following ESG rating announcements. Our analysis reveals significant positive (negative) abnormal returns of 1.42% (-1.89%) following ESG upgrades (downgrades) over a three-day event window. Importantly, we find that these effects are significantly amplified in states with stronger environmental policies, with an additional 0.67 percentage point effect in high-policy states. The policy moderation effect is strongest for environmental pillar changes and persists over longer event windows. Our findings contribute to the growing literature on ESG investing and highlight the important role of regulatory environments in shaping market responses to sustainability information.

3. Methodology

3.1 Event Study Framework

Abnormal Return Calculation:
$$AR_{i,t} = R_{i,t} - E[R_{i,t} | X_t]$$
Cumulative Abnormal Returns:
$$CAR_{i,[t_1,t_2]} = \sum_{t=t_1}^{t_2} AR_{i,t}$$

Factor Model Specifications

Carhart 4-Factor Model (Primary):

$$R_{i,t} - R_{f,t} = \alpha_i + \beta_{1i} MKT_t + \beta_{2i} SMB_t + \beta_{3i} HML_t + \beta_{4i} UMD_t + \epsilon_{i,t}$$
